Nootropics, often referred to as “smart drugs” or cognitive enhancers, are substances that purportedly improve cognitive function, particularly executive functions, memory, creativity, or motivation, in healthy individuals. They can be synthetic compounds or natural substances.

## Caffeine

### Cognitive function

Caffeine is effective in enhancing focus and attention, particularly under conditions of fatigue or sleep deprivation. Its effects on memory are more variable, showing benefits mainly in short-term and explicit memory tasks under specific conditions. The impact on motivation is present but generally modest. Overall, caffeine can be considered a useful aid for improving certain cognitive functions, especially focus and attention.

### Mental health

The relationship between caffeine and mental health is multifaceted. While moderate caffeine intake may have some benefits in reducing depressive symptoms and enhancing the effects of antidepressants, it can also increase anxiety, particularly in individuals with pre-existing anxiety disorders. High caffeine consumption is generally associated with higher anxiety levels and can exacerbate mood disorders. The overall impact of caffeine on depression and anxiety varies among individuals, and further research is needed to clarify these effects.

## L-Theanine

### Cognitive function

L-Theanine appears to enhance focus and working memory, particularly when combined with caffeine. It shows promise in improving selective attention and memory, especially in individuals with mild cognitive impairment. In children with ADHD, L-Theanine can improve attention and cognitive performance, though it may increase impulsivity if not combined with caffeine. Overall, L-Theanine, especially in combination with caffeine, is effective in improving various aspects of cognitive function.

### Mental health

The collective evidence suggests that L-Theanine supplementation can improve symptoms of depression and anxiety, enhance sleep quality, and boost cognitive functions. While it appears to be a safe and well-tolerated supplement, further long-term and placebo-controlled studies are needed to fully substantiate its efficacy and establish clinical guidelines for its use.
